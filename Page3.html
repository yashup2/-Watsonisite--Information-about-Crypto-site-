Cryptocurrency vs fiat currency

Since the rise of cryptocurrency, there has been much debate about which is better, cryptocurrency or fiat? Both have pros and cons, and figuring out which is better depends on the business using it.

Cryptocurrency Pros-



1) Cryptocurrencies are digital and global :


Let us say you own a business in a high-risk industry such as online gambling, pharmaceuticals or forex. You may need to make international payments on a regular basis and this can be expensive and time-consuming when using fiat money. In contrast, cryptocurrency allows you to make these transactions quickly and easily without worrying about the high fees charged by banks for cross-border payments.

2) Cryptocurrencies are pseudonymous :


cryptocurrency transactions are anonymous and pseudonymous. Once a transaction has been made, it is very difficult to trace it back to the sender or receiver. This makes cryptocurrency a great option for businesses that need to keep their transactions private, such as political campaigns or non-profit organisations.

3) Cryptocurrencies are secure :


Cryptocurrencies use blockchain technology, which is a decentralised and distributed ledger. This means that all transactions on the blockchain are recorded and can be verified, but they cannot be tampered with or changed.

4) Cryptocurrency transactions are fast :


cryptocurrency transactions can be verified and processed almost instantly, making it a great choice for businesses that need to make fast payments, such as those in the e-commerce industry.


Cryptocurrency cons-



1) Cryptocurrency is volatile :


The price of cryptocurrency can be very volatile. This means that the value of your investment may go up or down, and this can happen very quickly. For example, in December 2017, the price of Bitcoin surged to almost $20,000 before crashing to around $3,000 just a few months later.

2) Cryptocurrency is unregulated :


For example, if you are a company that deals with high-risk payment solutions, such as online gambling, you may need to comply with anti-money laundering (AML) regulations. However, because cryptocurrency is unregulated, it can be difficult to comply with these regulations.

3) Cryptocurrencies are not widely accepted :


At the moment, cryptocurrencies are not widely accepted as a form of payment. This means that you may have problems finding businesses that accept cryptocurrency, which can be a problem if you are trying to use it as your primary form of payment.


Fiat Pros-



1) Fiat is regulated :


One of the biggest advantages of using fiat money is that its regulated by the government. This means that businesses can be sure that they are compliant with the law, and they will not have to worry about breaking any regulations.

2) Fiat is widely accepted :


For example, if you are running an online store that sells consumer electronics, it is likely that most of your customers will be willing to use traditional payment methods like credit cards or bank transfers.

3) Fiat is stable :


While the price of cryptocurrencies can fluctuate dramatically, fiat currency is much more stable. This means that businesses can rely on being able to access a consistent amount of funding, which is essential for planning and forecasting.


Fiat cons-



1) Fiat payments can take time :


For example, if you are running a digital business and you want to accept bank transfers from your customers, you may have to wait several days for these payments to clear. In contrast, cryptocurrency payments can often be processed instantly.

2) Fiat is subjected to inflation :


For example, if you are running a business that deals with high-value goods, you may need to keep a large amount of cash on hand. However, if the value of fiat currency decreases, the value of your cash holdings will also decrease.

3) Fiat is subject to government control :


For example, if you are running a business that sells products online and you want to accept fiat payments from your customers, the government may have the power to shut down your website or block access to your funds. This can be a big problem for businesses that need to process transactions quickly.
